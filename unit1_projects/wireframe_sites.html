The Verge

On evaulation of the problem The Verge tries to solve I consider its aim is to engage people in news of specific areas, perhaps in an alternate way to other mainstream reporting companys such as those on TV or physical material-newspapers and their websites. On the 'About' section they state: "Its mission is to offer in-depth reporting and long-form feature stories, breaking news coverage, product information, and community content in a unified and cohesive manner."

Visual challenges of this website include just the sheer volume of information the website holds and gathers on a daily basis, meaning the archives are growing almost by the hour and trying to present the most important (trending, widely reported, or even under reported elsewhere) information in a visually cohesive manner without making the website too busy or overloading the user with images/videos/headlines. StumpleUpon

StumbleUpon aims to provide a solution to people wanting more knowledge and information about anything and everything they are interested in, from sports to design to travel, literally everyone can find content on their interests. This is what StumbleUpon have to say themselves: "StumbleUpon is great for finding you content you might never have otherwise found on your own."

Having external content presenting within the body of the website might create visual problems such as getting the selected website of interest to display correctly within the parameters of StumbleUpon's own website. Other than this they need to keep the home page with reccomendations very clean and clear, not unlike the verge, to keep the user from being overloaded.
